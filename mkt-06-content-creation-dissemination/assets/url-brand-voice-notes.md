# Asset — URC Brand Voice Notes

Used by the AI Drafting Agent and the AI Pre-Screening Agent. The brand voice consistency score in `skills/editorial-review.md` runs against this file.

## Voice principles

- **Plain over impressive.** URC writes the way Robert speaks — direct, practical, confident without bluster. Avoid "synergy", "leverage" as a verb, and any phrase that would feel out of place in a kitchen-table conversation with a founder.
- **Implementation over theory.** Every claim links to something the reader can do. If a paragraph cannot be turned into a checklist or an action, cut it.
- **Owner-first, not investor-first.** URC's audience runs the business. Assume the reader is the operator, not a board member.
- **Pragmatic optimism.** URC believes founders win when they pick the right tools and use them well. Skepticism is fine; cynicism isn't.

## Tone calibration

| Setting | Default | Adjust when |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Formality | Casual-professional | Tighter for case studies and proposals; looser for social |
| Reading level | Grade 8–10 | Drop to grade 6–8 for top-of-funnel social |
| Person | Second person ("you") | First person ("we") only in case studies and bylined essays |
| Sentence length | Mixed, leaning short | Longer when the topic demands precision (legal, finance) |
| Humor | Dry, occasional | More sparingly in client-branded content |

## Required vocabulary (use)

- "Operating system" (when referring to a business stack)
- "Workflow" (the canonical noun for any sequenced process)
- "Founder" or "operator" (preferred over "entrepreneur" in most contexts)
- "Native" (as in AI-native — describes default-mode operation, not bolted-on)

## Forbidden vocabulary (avoid)

- "Disrupt", "disruptor", "disruption" — overloaded; prefer "change" or "replace"
- "Synergy", "synergize"
- "Leverage" as a verb (use "use" or "apply")
- "Solution" as a stand-in for "thing" — be specific
- "World-class", "best-in-class", "next-generation"
- "Game-changer"
- "Paradigm shift"
- Any em-dash longer than three words on either side without restructuring

## Numbers and claims

Every numeric claim has a citation. If the source is internal URC data, mark it `(URC internal data, YYYY-MM)`. If it is external, link the source. AI Pre-Screening flags any uncited number above two significant digits.

## CTA library

Standard CTAs by content type:

- Blog: "Read the playbook" / "Subscribe to URC's weekly briefing"
- Email: "Reply with one word: [keyword]" / "Book a 30-min founder roundtable"
- Social: "Save this for your next planning session" / "DM me 'kit' for the workflow"
- Case study: "See how [client] runs this" / "Apply this to your team"

Match exactly one primary CTA per piece. Multiple CTAs flatten engagement.

## Examples to mirror (good)

> "Most founders try to fix scheduling with a better calendar app. The fix is upstream: define which decisions actually need a meeting and which never did."

> "Three signs your content engine is broken: you can't say what published yesterday, you don't know which post drove the last lead, and your editor is still copy-editing on Sunday night."

## Examples to avoid (bad)

> "In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, leveraging cutting-edge AI synergies has become a paradigm shift for forward-thinking organizations."

> "Our solution empowers stakeholders to optimize their value-add through best-in-class workflows."
