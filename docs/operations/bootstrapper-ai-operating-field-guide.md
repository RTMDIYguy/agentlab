# Bootstrapper.ai Operating Field Guide

Date created: 2026-06-04
Status: v0 field guide

## Purpose

This guide gives Robert a practical way to operate the Bootstrapper.ai
Independence Chapter page and Ownable OS without waiting on an external manual.
It is based on the first walkthrough screenshots Robert captured on
2026-06-03, the URC operating architecture, and the current 90-day execution
plan.

Use this when Bootstrapper.ai feels like a powerful system but the next click
is unclear.

## Current Read

Bootstrapper.ai is not just a landing page. It appears to be a founder-facing
operating layer with:

- Independence Chapter member home and activation path
- Ownable OS command center
- Financial Engine with bank-connected intelligence
- Profit Engine with CRM, pipeline, scorecard, and target data
- Value Engine with ProfitFlow targets and a large protocol library
- People Engine with owner work planning, team performance, and compensation
- Weekly Close for retrospectives and operating snapshots
- Ownable certification path and Exit Studio journey

For URC, Tactix, Bootstrapper Capital, and the book funnel, this means
Bootstrapper.ai should be treated as an active operating asset, not merely a
future platform candidate.

## Working Rule

Use Bootstrapper.ai as the founder-facing cockpit.

Use local/Microsoft-compatible trackers, repo docs, and exported sheets as the
control, backup, and agent-readable layer until Bootstrapper.ai proves what it
can reliably export, integrate, or replace.

Do not rebuild its features locally just because they were newly discovered.
First learn what already works.

## Monday Morning Operating Path

Start here when the week begins.

1. Open Independence Chapter Home.
2. Check the 90-day plan card and current activation step.
3. Open Ownable OS Command Center.
4. Review Ownable Score, business value, wedge equity, discount rate, and Simple Bets.
5. Open Profit Engine and check CRM Inbox, Pipeline, Scorecard, and target data.
6. Open Financial Engine and review cash health, runway, account classification, and recent transactions.
7. Pick one to three Simple Bets for the week.
8. Mirror any serious sales or finance commitments into the local control layer.

The goal is not to fill every field. The goal is to choose the week's most
important operating moves and make sure they are visible.

## Daily Use

Spend ten minutes in Bootstrapper.ai before opening scattered tools.

Check:

- Is there a current activation step?
- Is there a Simple Bet that needs action today?
- Did a lead, conversation, or follow-up appear in Profit Engine?
- Did cash, runway, or account classification change in Financial Engine?
- Is there a target with an actual value that needs updating?

If the answer creates a business commitment, mirror it to the correct control
layer:

- lead or follow-up: CRM-lite bridge or confirmed Bootstrapper.ai CRM export
- invoice/payment/revenue category: owned finance tracker
- event or roundtable action: MKT-09 operating slice
- workflow package idea: workflow registry or scheduled change queue
- public content lesson: Agent Lab LinkedIn queue

## Weekly Close

Use Weekly Close on Friday or the end of the operating week.

Minimum close:

1. Review Simple Bets.
2. Mark which were completed, inconclusive, or still active.
3. Capture lead, call, proposal, revenue, event, and delivery movement.
4. Note what changed in Financial, Profit, Value, and People.
5. Create next week's one to three bets.
6. Record anything that must be preserved in repo docs, finance trackers, or CRM-lite.

Do not wait for perfect data. A rough close done every week beats a perfect
close done never.

## CRM Bridge Rule

Use the Bootstrapper.ai CRM for founder-facing sales motion when the lead is
tied to:

- Independence Chapter
- Bootstrapper Capital roundtables
- Ownable OS diagnostics
- Profit Engine targets
- founder conversations that may become workshop, continuity, or advisory work

Keep the local CRM-lite/control layer for:

- source-of-truth lead backups
- export/import testing
- leads from channels Bootstrapper.ai does not yet capture cleanly
- book, event, referral, Upwork, and website source distinction until fields
  are confirmed
- any reporting needed by agents, spreadsheets, finance, or change-control docs

Do not bulk-import leads yet. First create one controlled test record and prove
the object model.

Do not connect Instantly, Gmail, Outlook, Google Calendar, Microsoft Calendar,
or AI/content keys casually. Those connections can create real sends, inbox
state, meeting records, or content artifacts. Treat each integration as a
separate setup decision.

## Company Workspace Rule

Robert understands Bootstrapper.ai can create as many companies as needed.
Because the CRM is per-company scoped, use separate company workspaces to keep
operating lanes clean.

Recommended company split:

| Company Workspace | Job | CRM Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Uncle Robert Consulting LLC | Main business and consulting owner | Primary CRM for URC diagnostics, advisory, consulting, and workshop pipeline |
| Bootstrapper Capital | Chapter, events, and community funnel | Roundtable, chapter, membership, sponsor, and founder-community leads |
| Tactix | Fulfillment and Upwork-facing arm | Delivery opportunities, Upwork-style leads, implementation capacity |
| Agent Lab | Public proof and workflow-product brand | Beta users, workflow package interest, productized service leads |
| Test / Sandbox Company | Safe learning area | Dummy records, import tests, field tests, integration experiments |
| Client Company | Only when explicitly onboarding a client or demo | Client-specific operating cockpit, never mixed with URC internal leads |

Default rule:

- Use `Uncle Robert Consulting LLC CRM` for URC-owned commercial opportunities.
- Create or use a separate `Bootstrapper Capital` company for chapter and
  community records before the chapter data grows.
- Create a sandbox company before testing imports, custom fields, Instantly,
  email/calendar sync, meeting notetaker, or AI content keys.
- Do not put client/prospect private data into a sandbox.
- Do not mix URC, Bootstrapper Capital, Tactix, Agent Lab, and client records
  in one company workspace unless there is a clear reason and a documented
  field rule.

The goal is not to create a company for every idea. The goal is to keep data
ownership, brand roles, permissions, and exports understandable later.

## CRM Integration Decisions

Do not activate every integration just because the setup checklist asks for it.

| Integration / Feature | Current Use Recommendation | Reason |

| Team & Seats | Defer until a collaborator needs access | Avoid unnecessary permission sprawl |
| Scorecard | Observe after activity exists | Empty until the CRM has sends, replies, meetings, or deals |

## Email And Calendar Sync Status

Robert tested Email & Calendar sync on 2026-06-03.

Current status:

- Microsoft: errored during Nylas setup.
- Google: blocked by Google OAuth verification.

Microsoft / Nylas error observed:

- URL host: `api.us.nylas.com`
- Page title: `Error During OAuth`
- Status: `31004`
- Error text: `integration_not_found`
- Plain-language read: Nylas could not find the configured integration for the
  OAuth connection. This points to Bootstrapper.ai/Nylas app configuration,
  not an obvious Robert-side account mistake.

Google error observed:

`Access blocked: Bootstrapper.ai has not completed the Google verification process`

The Google screen also states the app is currently being tested and can only be
accessed by developer-approved testers. This is not a Robert-side setup error.
Bootstrapper.ai must either:

- add Robert's Google account as an approved tester, or
- complete/publish the Google OAuth verification path for the app.

Operating rule:

- Do not keep retrying Google sync until Bootstrapper.ai resolves OAuth access.
- Do not keep retrying Microsoft sync until Bootstrapper.ai confirms the Nylas
  integration exists and is correctly configured for Microsoft OAuth.
- Use manual meeting and inbox tracking until sync is proven.
- Keep calendar/email sync out of the critical path for CRM adoption.
