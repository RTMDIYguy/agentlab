## Executive Summary

Uncle Robert Consulting LLC is a hands-on AI consulting and workflow systems company founded and operated by Robert T. McCarthy. The company helps small businesses, solo founders, and content-driven operators turn scattered tools, inconsistent follow-up, and founder-dependent processes into practical AI-assisted operating systems.

URC's current operating thesis is simple: small businesses do not need another pile of disconnected software. They need clear workflows, accountable follow-up, useful automation, and systems they can keep running after the consultant leaves.

Agent Lab is the workflow product and AI systems layer being built from that consulting practice. Its early product direction is the Founder Signal System: a lightweight marketing and lead-generation workflow that helps founders clarify who they serve, create useful content, run human-reviewed outreach, capture proof, and book better conversations.

The long-term platform vision is an AI-native agency operating system: seven business departments, mapped workflows, playbooks, automation assets, trained agents, and a central orchestration layer that helps humans make strategic decisions while AI handles repeatable operational work.

## Mission

To work alongside clients, not merely advise them, building the AI systems, automations, and playbooks that turn chaotic small businesses into sustainable, scalable operations.

URC does not disappear after the invoice. The work continues until the system functions.

## Vision

Uncle Robert Consulting is the hands-on partner small businesses and content creators trust to build AI-powered systems that work, so their businesses run more reliably, generate income more predictably, and stop depending on the founder being on call at all times.

The longer-term vision is an AI-native agency platform where seven departments are mapped into workflows, playbooks, and trained AI agents, coordinated by a central orchestration layer under human direction.

## Problem

Small businesses and solo founders often have more tools than operating clarity.

Common symptoms include:

- leads coming from multiple places without one follow-up process
- content being published without a clear buyer, message, or proof loop
- founders losing momentum when client work gets busy
- manual work repeating because no one has written the workflow down
- automation being added before the business process is clear
- public marketing, CRM, finance, and fulfillment records living in separate systems
- no reliable way for a new human or agent to continue the work

The result is not just inefficiency. The founder becomes the operating system.

## Solution

URC / Agent Lab solves this by turning recurring business activity into documented, testable, AI-assisted workflows.

The working model:

1. identify the practical business outcome
2. map the workflow
3. define the human approval points
4. create the templates, prompts, trackers, and automation assets
5. test the workflow with real work
6. package the workflow for reuse
7. improve the workflow through change control and proof capture

This approach lets the business automate the repeatable parts while keeping human judgment over trust-sensitive actions.

## Current Flagship Workflow: Founder Signal System

The Founder Signal System is a starter marketing and lead-generation workflow for founders who already have customers but cannot tell whether their message, content, or outreach is working.

The system gives the founder:

- a one-page signal brief
- a repeatable content, outreach, follow-up, and proof cycle
- a first published or prepared batch of content
- a reviewed outreach list
- a three-touch follow-up sequence
- a proof log for qualified conversations and market signal

The system is intentionally not a generic social scheduler. It is a founder signal workflow: message clarity, human-reviewed outreach, useful content, and evidence of what creates real conversations.

## Technology And Systems

The business follows a one-system-per-job principle.

Current system roles:

- B12-hosted Agent Lab site for public marketing, product pages, blog, and beta surfaces
- Supabase for authentication, database, Edge Functions, blog/admin functions, and future beta app data
- Stripe for planned subscription and checkout management
- Microsoft 365 / OneDrive as the preferred office and file backbone when available
- Frappe as the finance system of record
- Notion as a lightweight dashboard and index only
- n8n for local automation, proof lanes, and workflow certification tests
- GitHub / local repo folders for workflow packages, operating docs, change control, and source files
- CRM-lite trackers for leads, next actions, follow-up dates, and stage visibility
- Gmail, Reach, LinkedIn, and manual review for early outreach

The system is intentionally designed to continue operating during tool constraints, paid-access interruptions, or platform changes.

## Governance

URC / Agent Lab uses human-led, AI-assisted governance.

AI agents may draft, research, prepare, test, log, and recommend. Robert retains approval over external sends, public publishing, money movement, client-visible commitments, access changes, and authoritative business records.

Meaningful changes are recorded in the change-control register. Non-urgent work is scheduled instead of distracting from testing, certification, packaging, and market execution.

Current cadence:

- weekly review: Friday at 9:00 AM America/Chicago
- monthly review: 1st day of each month
- quarterly review: January, April, July, October
- annual close: December

## Key Risks And Mitigations

Risk: tool sprawl and duplicated systems.

Mitigation: use one system per job, keep Notion lightweight, and avoid paid tools unless they clearly save time or create revenue.

Risk: over-automation before proof.

Mitigation: document and run workflows manually first; automate only after the process and approval gates are clear.

Risk: public promises moving faster than delivery capacity.

Mitigation: use human approval for publishing, offers, pricing, and client-visible commitments.

Risk: business memory trapped in one founder or one AI session.

Mitigation: maintain source documents, SOPs, change control, workflow packages, and evidence logs in durable files.

Risk: technology blockers in early SaaS development.

Mitigation: use manual and low-cost bridges, log blockers, and keep the product wedge narrow until the system proves itself.

## Strategic Thesis

URC / Agent Lab is building the product by doing the work.

The consulting practice funds the platform. Each client engagement, workflow package, automation proof, and operating document becomes part of the eventual AI-native operating system.

The near-term business does not require pretending the platform is fully built. The strength of the company is that it is honest about its stage while continuing to ship usable systems.
