# URC / Agent Lab Governance SOP

Date prepared: 2026-05-24
Status: Presentable v1
Owner: Robert T. McCarthy
Applies to: Uncle Robert Consulting, Agent Lab, Tactix, Bootstrapper Capital, and Bootstrapper's Guide to the World

## Purpose

This SOP defines how Uncle Robert Consulting and Agent Lab govern work, approvals, workflow changes, public publishing, and AI-assisted execution.

The standard is simple: automate what can be safely prepared, keep human judgment over commitments, and preserve trust with clients, partners, prospects, and public audiences.

## Governance Principle

URC / Agent Lab operates with human-led, AI-assisted governance.

AI agents may draft, research, prepare, test, log, and recommend. Robert retains authority over decisions that affect money, access, public commitments, client trust, published material, or authoritative business records.

The operating goal is not more bureaucracy. The goal is to make work clear enough that a human or agent can continue it without guessing.

## Scope

This SOP applies to:

- internal operating changes
- workflow package changes
- public content and publishing
- website and SaaS beta updates
- lead-generation and CRM-lite updates
- client-facing or prospect-facing commitments
- finance, invoicing, and payment status records
- system, account, or access changes
- SOP, evidence, and change-control records

## Governance Cadence

Weekly review:

- Day: Friday
- Time: 9:00 AM
- Timezone: America/Chicago
- Focus: finance, KPIs, active leads, workflow progress, open risks, and next-week setup

Monthly review:

- Day: 1st day of the month
- Focus: revenue, pipeline, offers, tool spend, and active priorities

Quarterly review:

- Starts: January, April, July, October
- Focus: SOP review, offer ladder, workflow packaging, automation readiness, and system cleanup

Annual close:

- Month: December
- Focus: mission, vision, annual performance, platform direction, and strategic commitments

## Decision Rights

Robert must approve:

- external sends
- public publishing
- paid offers, pricing, or material commercial terms
- client-visible commitments
- money movement, invoicing decisions, or payment-status changes
- access, permissions, credentials, or account changes
- authoritative changes to SOPs, workflow packages, operating architecture, or business records
- material brand hierarchy changes among URC, Agent Lab, Tactix, Bootstrapper Capital, or the book

Agents may prepare:

- draft content
- lead research
- CRM-lite updates for review
- workflow documentation
- SOP drafts
- change-control entries
- testing evidence
- implementation plans
- diagnostic summaries
- recommendations and next actions

Agents may not independently commit the business externally.

## Approval Gates

Use owner approval before any of the following:

1. Sending a message to a prospect, client, partner, or public audience.
2. Publishing or materially updating public website, blog, email, or social content.
3. Changing prices, offer promises, terms, or refund expectations.
4. Adding, removing, or changing account access.
5. Moving money or changing finance records.
6. Changing authoritative business records, workflow packages, or SOPs.
7. Making a client-visible delivery commitment.

## Change Control

Meaningful changes are recorded in:

`docs/operations/change-control-register.md`

Non-urgent changes are scheduled in:

`docs/operations/scheduled-change-queue.md`

Fast-lane changes are limited to work that directly unblocks:

- workflow testing
- workflow certification
- workflow packaging
- release readiness
- market-facing execution

## Evidence Standard

Operational proof should be captured close to the artifact when local placement is required for testing or debugging.

Cross-workflow evidence, compliance notes, audits, certification logs, and readiness records should be stored in:

`C:\Users\thebo\OneDrive - Uncle Robert Consulting LLC\Working Docs\AI Native Agency Deepened\Compliance Audits`

Evidence should answer four questions:

1. What changed?
2. Why did it change?
3. What proves it works or what remains blocked?
4. Who approved or performed the change?

## SOP Standard

Every active SOP should include:

- Owner
- Last updated date
- Version or status
- Purpose
- Scope
- Tools and inputs
- Step-by-step procedure
- Outputs and success criteria
- Version history or change-control reference

SOPs should remain short enough to use while doing the work.

## Operating Rules

1. Use existing systems before adding new tools.
2. Preserve existing business data.
3. Keep URC as the main business brand.
4. Keep Bootstrapper Capital as the audience and event funnel.
5. Keep Tactix as the fulfillment and Upwork arm.
6. Treat the book as an authority and funnel asset.
7. Use Microsoft 365 as the preferred operating backbone when available.
8. Use free or low-cost bridges when paid tools are unavailable or unjustified.
9. Keep Notion as a lightweight dashboard, not the main archive.
10. Keep Frappe as the finance system of record.
11. Automate first, review exceptions, and use paid human help only where judgment, compliance, or leverage requires it.
12. Do not redesign the business from zero.

## Success Criteria

Governance is working when:

- important decisions are not trapped in memory
- public and client-facing work has human approval
- agents can continue work from source documents
- lead, content, finance, and workflow changes are visible
- meaningful changes are logged
- non-urgent work is scheduled instead of distracting from market execution
- tools have one clear job
- the weekly cadence is actually followed
