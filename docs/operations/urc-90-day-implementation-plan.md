# URC 90-Day Implementation Plan

## Purpose

This plan translates the v1 operating architecture into a practical 90-day sequence for:

- URC
- Tactix
- Bootstrapper Capital
- Bootstrapper's Guide to the World

The goal is to move from fragmented assets to a consistent operating rhythm.

## Phase 1: Days 1-30

### Objectives

- confirm the operating backbone
- simplify the system
- establish the first live control layer

### Actions

1. Confirm the tool split.

- `Microsoft 365` for communication, docs, file storage, collaboration, and forms
- `Frappe` for invoices and finance records
- `Notion` only for lightweight dashboards and planning

2. Create the minimum business structure.

- `URC` as main business
- `Bootstrapper Capital` as funnel and community
- `Tactix` as fulfillment and Upwork arm

3. Create one file structure in OneDrive.

- URC
- Bootstrapper Capital
- Tactix
- Finance
- Sales
- Marketing
- Operations
- Client Delivery
- Book
- Archive

4. Build one executive dashboard.

Track:

- leads
- proposals
- active clients
- invoices due
- content status
- events
- book funnel activity

5. Define the offer ladder.

- book
- free founder session or roundtable
- workshop or bootcamp
- monthly continuity offer
- consulting sprint or advisory

6. Clarify the job of the book.

The book should feed:

- trust
- community entry
- events
- advisory

### Outputs

- one operating map
- one dashboard
- one offer ladder
- one clarified book CTA

## Phase 2: Days 31-60

### Objectives

- launch a repeatable founder-facing funnel
- standardize pipeline and follow-up
- connect content to offers

### Actions

1. Launch one founder-facing funnel.

Use:

- LinkedIn and Instagram content
- CTA to a form or landing page
- invitation to one live event or roundtable
- follow-up into workshop, continuity, or consulting

2. Create one recurring event rhythm.

Choose one:

- weekly founder roundtable
- biweekly strategy session
- monthly bootstrapper workshop

3. Build a simple CRM-lite.

Track:

- lead
- source
- interest
- status
- next step
- follow-up date

4. Clarify the role of Tactix.

Use it for:

- Upwork lead capture
- freelance fulfillment
- overflow implementation capacity

5. Standardize invoicing in Frappe.

Ensure:

- every engagement is invoiced from one place
- receivables are visible
- basic revenue reporting exists

6. Turn the book into content fuel.

Create:

- short LinkedIn posts
- Instagram content
- founder lessons
- event invitations tied to book ideas

### Outputs

- one functioning funnel
- one recurring event
- one CRM-lite tracker
- one finance rhythm
- one book content stream

## Phase 3: Days 61-90

### Objectives

- monetize continuity
- create repeatable conversion
- identify what to scale

### Actions

1. Launch the monthly continuity offer.

Possible components:

- office hours
- group Q and A
- accountability
- planning support
- light implementation guidance

2. Launch one flagship workshop or bootcamp.

Use a clear problem and clear result.

3. Build a referral loop.

At the end of events and client work, ask for:

- testimonial
- referral
- introduction
- workshop invite

4. Review monthly performance.

Measure:

- leads
- event attendance
- proposals
- invoices paid
- continuity members
- consulting closes
- book-driven inquiries

5. Decide what to scale.

Focus only on channels that are showing traction.

### Outputs

- one continuity offer
- one flagship workshop
- one referral mechanism
- one monthly review rhythm

## Weekly Rhythm

### Monday

- sales and pipeline

### Tuesday

- marketing and book promotion

### Wednesday

- Bootstrapper Capital and events

### Thursday

- fulfillment and aftercare

### Friday

- finance and KPI review

## Key Metrics

- leads added
- discovery calls booked
- proposals sent
- invoices due
- invoices paid
- event RSVPs
- event attendance
- continuity members
- book-driven inquiries
- delivered outcomes

## Rules For Execution

- do not add more platforms unless there is a clear operational gap
- do not let URC, Tactix, and Bootstrapper Capital compete for identity
- do not treat the book as a standalone product with no next step
- do not let Notion become the archive
- favor consistency over complexity

## End-State At Day 90

By day 90, the business should have:

- a clear brand hierarchy
- a working weekly rhythm
- a visible lead and sales process
- a functioning event or founder funnel
- a defined role for the book
- a continuity offer
- cleaner separation between active systems and archives
