## Core Concept

This book should read like a mentor taking a new startup founder under his wing
and teaching him the ropes of building a successful, sustainable business.

The desired feel is:

- `How to Win Friends and Influence People` in readability and story-led
  teaching
- `Ownable` in practical systems thinking, agency, clarity, and implementation
- Uncle Robert in voice: warm, direct, patient, practical, relational,
  truth-seeking, and willing to build beside the reader

The reader should feel:

- seen
- taught
- encouraged
- challenged without being shamed
- clearer about what to do next
- accompanied by a mentor rather than lectured by an expert

## Narrative Premise

Uncle Robert meets a new founder who has energy, vision, and grit, but whose
business is starting to feel heavier than expected.

The founder is not failing. He is growing into a stage where personal effort is
no longer enough. Uncle Robert helps him see the business as an operating
system, then teaches him how to build one piece at a time.

The book should not invent a fake case study with made-up revenue, metrics, or
customer outcomes. Instead, it can use a composite founder character and
illustrative scenes to make the lessons readable.

## Suggested Mentor / Founder Frame

### Mentor

Uncle Robert:

- experienced but still learning
- practical teacher
- collaborative partner
- systems builder
- servant-leadership oriented
- willing to say, "I do not know yet; let us find out"
- honest that we update as we grow

### Founder

Composite founder name:

- Sam

The founder should represent:

- early-stage or small service business owner
- too many tools and too little clarity
- learning that structure is not the enemy of creativity

Sam should stay focused enough for readers to recognize themselves without
becoming a fake case study.

Use a composite disclaimer:

> The founder scenes in this book are composites. They are designed to teach
> practical lessons without exposing private client details.

Use an AI-assisted writing disclaimer:

> This book was developed with AI-assisted drafting and editing under Robert
> McCarthy's direction. The ideas, judgment, voice, final editorial decisions,
> and responsibility for the work remain with the author.

Use an AI-assisted graphics disclaimer when visuals are added:

> Some graphics or visual aids in this book may be AI-assisted. They are used
> to clarify concepts, not to represent real clients, private business records,
> or documented case results unless explicitly stated.

## Voice Rules

Use:

- "Hey partner" sparingly, mainly at openings or important turns
- "we" language
- direct questions
- warm correction
- short paragraphs
- plain examples
- practical next steps
- humility when truth changes
- repeated emphasis on learning, updating, transparency, and sincerity

Avoid:

- textbook distance
- fake certainty
- over-polished corporate voice
- dense lists in the main flow
- long diagnostic blocks without narrative context
- pretending every business problem has a simple software fix

## Chapter Pattern

Each chapter should follow this pattern:

1. **Scene:** the founder encounters a real business friction.
2. **Mentor conversation:** Uncle Robert names what is really happening.
3. **Principle:** explain the operating lesson in plain language.
4. **Practice:** show how to apply it.
5. **Operator checkpoint:** convert the lesson into a short tool, checklist, or
   reflection.
6. **Narrative return:** show how the founder sees the business differently
   after applying the lesson.
7. **Chapter takeaway:** one memorable sentence.

### Chapter 2: Removing the Weight from the Work

Scene idea:

## Calibre / Amazon KDP Production Constraints

The manuscript should be built with publication in mind from the beginning.
After drafting, the book will move into Calibre 64 for formatting, metadata
management, cover handling, and export preparation. From there, it should be
prepared for Amazon KDP review.

### Production Defaults

Working production assumption:

- Primary edition: Kindle eBook
- Secondary edition: paperback after the manuscript is stable
- Working print trim size: 6" x 9" unless Robert chooses otherwise
- Interior graphics: use only when they teach, summarize, or clarify
- Interior graphics style: consistent, simple, readable in grayscale, and
  reproducible across ebook and print
- Cover graphics: separate production asset, not embedded as a duplicate cover
  page unless the final ebook format requires an internal cover image

### Metadata Standard

All manuscript, cover, graphic, and export files should use the same core
metadata:

- Title: Startup Operational Excellence
- Subtitle: TBD
- Author: Robert McCarthy
- Publisher / imprint: Uncle Robert Consulting LLC, unless changed before
  publication
- Series: TBD, if this becomes part of a broader Uncle Robert / Ownable OS
  business series
- Language: English
- Edition: Draft until publication-ready
- AI-assisted writing disclosure: included in front/back matter
- AI-assisted graphics disclosure: included if graphics are used
- Composite character disclosure: included because Sam is a teaching composite

### Graphics Standard

All graphics should have:

- consistent visual style
- clear file names
- matching embedded or sidecar metadata where possible
- alt text or descriptive captions for ebook accessibility
- no fake client data
- no private business records
- no unlicensed stock assets
- no misleading AI-generated "case result" imagery
- source notes identifying whether the image is human-created, AI-assisted, or
  generated from a diagram/checklist

For Kindle eBook production, images should be high resolution and tested in
Kindle Previewer or KDP Previewer before upload. For print, avoid relying on
fine text inside images unless the final print proof confirms readability.

### KDP Readiness Notes

Current official KDP guidance requires attention to:

- eBook cover image dimensions, format, and quality
- internal cover image handling so the cover does not duplicate incorrectly
- print trim size, bleed, and margins
- paperback cover PDF requirements, including full cover spread when using a
  custom print cover
- page count, spine, and margin implications for paperback
- consistency between manuscript metadata, cover text, and Amazon detail page

The production pass must verify current KDP requirements again before final
export because Amazon standards can change.

### Calibre Handoff Rule

Before importing into Calibre 64, create a clean production folder containing:

- final source DOCX
- ebook-ready EPUB or Calibre source file
- cover image
- graphics folder
- metadata sheet
- disclaimer text
- KDP checklist
- export notes

Do not mix rough drafts, unused graphics, or earlier chapter-only files into
the Calibre production folder.
