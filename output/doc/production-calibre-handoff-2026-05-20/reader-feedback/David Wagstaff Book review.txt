David Wagstaff: Book review

First off, congratulations!!

Writing and finishing a book is a huge accomplishment, and I believe this is your 2nd. it’s clear you’ve put a lot of thought, experience, and care into this. I can already tell this comes from years of mentoring founders and genuinely wanting to help people build better businesses.

A few friendly first-impression thoughts as I skimmed the opening pages:

The biggest thing I noticed is that your social content has a lot of personality, warmth, and memorable visuals — especially the engaging graphics and even the dog character you sometimes use — but the cover currently feels much more corporate and subdued. I wonder if the cover could better reflect the energy and humanity that comes through in your posts and mentoring style. Right now the title also gets a bit lost visually.

I also found myself wanting a stronger emotional hook earlier. The practical wisdom is clearly there, but the opening sequence starts with legal/disclosure material and then moves quickly into operational problems founders face. As a reader, I wanted something that immediately pulled me in emotionally or made me curious before the “instruction” side began.

You clearly care deeply about helping founders, but I think there’s an opportunity to make readers feel inspired and understood before they feel analyzed or corrected. 

The strongest business books often make the reader think:
“This author gets me.”
before they think:
“This author is going to teach me.”

You may already be doing that later in the book, I only skimmed the beginning, but first impressions matter a lot in getting people emotionally invested enough to keep reading.

Overall though, I think the positioning is promising, especially because there’s authenticity behind it. It feels like it comes from lived experience rather than theory, and that’s valuable.
